Getting Started

Do you offer free consultations?

Yes. Every new client begins with a free, no-obligation consultation so we can understand your facility, operational needs, cleaning priorities, and scheduling requirements before recommending a customized cleaning program.

Why don't you provide pricing online?

Every facility is different. Building size, occupancy, operating hours, security requirements, floor surfaces, washrooms, and many other factors influence the scope of work. Rather than providing generic estimates, we prepare customized proposals after learning more about your facility.

How do I request a cleaning proposal?

Complete our Service Inquiry form. We'll review the information, contact you if additional details are needed, and arrange a consultation or site assessment when appropriate before preparing a customized proposal.

Will someone visit our facility before providing a proposal?

When appropriate, yes. Site visits help us understand the layout, traffic patterns, access requirements, scheduling considerations, and cleaning expectations so we can prepare an accurate proposal.

What information should we prepare before contacting you?

Helpful information includes your facility type, services requested, preferred cleaning frequency, building size, preferred schedule, security or access requirements, and any areas needing special attention. If you don't know all of this yet, we'll guide you through it.

How long does the consultation process usually take?

Every facility is different. Smaller projects may only require a brief discussion, while larger facilities may require additional conversations and a site assessment before a proposal is prepared.

Do I need to know exactly what services I need before contacting you?

No. Many clients contact us before defining the exact scope of work. We'll help identify your priorities and recommend a cleaning program that fits your facility.

Starting a New Cleaning Program

What happens when we switch from another cleaning company?

We'll review your current program, discuss expectations, identify priority areas, and gather site-specific information. For most ongoing commercial contracts we recommend beginning with an initial deep cleaning to establish a consistent baseline.

Why do you recommend an initial deep cleaning?

An initial deep cleaning establishes your facility to a consistent standard before routine maintenance begins. It allows ongoing janitorial services to maintain cleanliness rather than continually catching up.

Related Questions

Will we still need deep cleaning after that?

In most cases, yes. Routine maintenance preserves cleanliness while periodic deep cleaning addresses accumulated buildup. Future deep cleanings are usually less extensive because the building is maintained consistently.

Does every facility need the same level of deep cleaning?

No. Recommendations depend on occupancy, traffic, surfaces, washroom usage, seasonal conditions, and the overall condition of the facility.

Does every facility need the same cleaning schedule?

No. We'll recommend a schedule based on occupancy, traffic, operating hours, and your organization's needs rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.

Can your recommendations change over time?

Absolutely. As your organization evolves, we review your cleaning program and recommend practical improvements that enhance efficiency, consistency, and service quality.

Will we receive the same cleaning team?

Whenever possible, yes. Consistency helps our team become familiar with your facility and expectations while improving service quality.

Can you recommend changes to our current cleaning program?

Yes. We'll review your existing scope of work and, if appropriate, recommend improvements to efficiency, consistency, sustainability, or service quality.

Will you simply follow our existing cleaning program?

If you're satisfied with your current program, we'll work within those expectations. If we identify opportunities for improvement, we'll discuss them with you before any changes are made.

Can our cleaning program be adjusted after service begins?

Yes. During the first few visits we evaluate how the program performs in the real environment. Any recommendations are discussed with you before implementation.

Why might you recommend changes after the first few visits?

Some operational details only become apparent once routine service begins. We use those observations to refine workflows and improve long-term consistency.

How do you determine what our facility actually needs?

Recommendations are based on consultations, site assessments, discussions with your team, and observations during the first few visits. We build programs around how your facility is actually used.

Customized Cleaning Programs

Every facility is different, and no two organizations operate exactly the same way. Rather than offering standardized cleaning packages, we develop customized cleaning programs based on your facility, occupancy, operational needs, schedule, and long-term goals.

What types of facilities do you clean?

We provide customized commercial cleaning services for offices, public facilities, community organizations, property management companies, retail businesses, places of worship, recreational facilities, and residential clients. Every cleaning program is tailored to the facility.

Can our cleaning program be customized?

Absolutely. Programs are built around your building, priorities, occupancy, schedule, and expectations. Services may include routine janitorial cleaning, periodic deep cleaning, floor care, washroom sanitation, consumable restocking, post-construction cleaning, and other specialty services.

Scheduling & Service Management

Clear communication and thoughtful planning help us remain flexible while maintaining reliable service.

Can we request changes to our cleaning schedule?

Yes. Minor adjustments are generally easier with about 24 hours' notice, medium changes with about 48 hours' notice, and major changes such as adding buildings with approximately two weeks' notice whenever possible.

Related Questions

  • Can we temporarily pause our cleaning program?
  • What happens if we need to cancel a scheduled service?
  • Do you offer emergency response services?
  • How do you balance flexibility with consistent service?
Do you offer emergency response services?

Depending on staffing availability and operational capacity, emergency or short-notice cleaning may be available. Some commercial or public-sector clients may also establish on-call service arrangements as part of their agreement.

Can we reschedule a scheduled cleaning visit?

Yes. Whenever possible, we appreciate at least 48 hours' notice to help coordinate staffing efficiently.

What happens if our facility is unexpectedly closed?

Please notify us as soon as possible. We'll discuss the most appropriate option, including rescheduling or adjusting service.

What happens if we need to cancel a scheduled service?

Whenever possible, please provide at least 24 hours' notice. Late cancellations may be subject to cancellation fees depending on staffing commitments and notice provided.

Can we temporarily pause our cleaning program?

Yes. We can temporarily pause services for renovations, holidays, seasonal operations, or other business needs with advance notice whenever possible.

Can cleaning services increase during busy periods?

Absolutely. We can often increase service frequency for inspections, events, renovations, or periods of higher occupancy.

Can cleaning services be reduced if our operational needs change?

Yes. If occupancy decreases or areas are closed, we'll review the program and recommend appropriate adjustments.

Do you clean during evenings, weekends, or overnight?

Yes. Depending on staffing, facility requirements, and the agreed scope of work, services can often be scheduled outside normal business hours.

How often should we review our cleaning program?

We recommend reviewing your program whenever operational needs change and periodically even when they do not to ensure it continues delivering the best value.

What if our operational needs change after the contract begins?

Organizations evolve over time. We'll gladly review your cleaning program and recommend practical adjustments that continue supporting your goals.

How do you balance flexibility with consistent service?

Through planning, communication, documentation, and ongoing collaboration. Our goal is to adapt when needed while maintaining reliable, consistent service.

Working Together

A successful cleaning program is built on more than schedules and checklists. Clear communication, documented expectations, and a collaborative approach help us deliver consistent service while adapting to the changing needs of your facility.

Will we have the same cleaning team?

Whenever possible, yes. Maintaining consistency helps our team become familiar with your facility, routines, and expectations. When staffing changes are necessary, we work to ensure a smooth transition through site-specific guidance and documented procedures.

Can we provide site-specific instructions?

Absolutely. During onboarding we'll document site-specific procedures, access instructions, priority areas, security requirements, and operational considerations. To maintain consistency, ongoing changes to the scope of work or site procedures should be communicated through our management team rather than directly to cleaning staff.

Who should we contact if we'd like to change our cleaning program?

Please contact our management team. Changes to scope, schedules, service frequency, or site procedures are reviewed, documented, and communicated through management to help maintain consistency and prevent misunderstandings.

Why shouldn't we request additional work directly from cleaners?

Our team is always happy to help, but changes to the agreed scope of work should be coordinated through management. This helps prevent scope creep, ensures requests are properly reviewed, and keeps expectations consistent for every visit.

How do you communicate with clients?

Communication is an important part of every cleaning program. We encourage regular communication regarding schedule changes, questions, feedback, and service adjustments. When appropriate, we'll also recommend practical improvements that may enhance efficiency, consistency, or service quality.

Can your team work while employees or visitors are present?

Yes. Many facilities remain occupied during scheduled cleaning. Our team works professionally and respectfully to minimize disruption while maintaining a safe and clean environment.

Can your team work in secure facilities?

Yes. We regularly work in facilities with controlled access and site-specific operational requirements. Before service begins, we'll review any access instructions and security procedures needed for your facility.

How do you protect confidential information?

Our team respects client confidentiality, follows site-specific instructions, and understands the importance of protecting sensitive information encountered while working in occupied facilities.

How do you maintain consistent service quality?

Consistency is built through preparation, communication, documented procedures, and continuous improvement. As we become familiar with your facility, we periodically review the cleaning program and recommend adjustments whenever appropriate.

Do you guarantee your work?

Yes. We stand behind the quality of our work. If you believe something has been missed or wasn't completed as expected, please contact our management team as soon as reasonably possible after the scheduled service. We'll review the concern and, when appropriate, return to address it. Prompt reporting helps us investigate before normal building activity affects the area.

Security & Facility Access

Many of the facilities we maintain require controlled access, security procedures, or site-specific protocols. We work with each client to establish clear access procedures before services begin.

How are keys, alarm codes, and access information managed?

Access information is coordinated through management during onboarding and only shared with authorized team members when required to perform scheduled services. We encourage clients to notify management whenever access procedures change.

Can your team lock and secure our facility after service?

Yes. When included as part of the agreed cleaning program and site procedures, our team can secure the facility after service by following your documented closing procedures.

What happens if our access procedures change?

Can site access instructions be updated?
