Public Facility Cleaning

Public & Municipal Facility Cleaning

Public facility cleaning is designed for buildings that serve residents, staff, and visitors on an ongoing basis, including municipal offices, community buildings, and similar public-facing spaces. These facilities often see steadier public traffic than a typical office, and cleaning programs are built with that continuous use, and the accountability that comes with it, in mind.

Cleaning support for municipal, public-sector, and community-serving facilities throughout Sarnia-Lambton.

Typical Facilities

Where this service is commonly provided.

Public facility cleaning applies to buildings that regularly welcome members of the public alongside staff.

  • Municipal & administrative buildings
  • Community centres & halls
  • Public-facing government offices
  • Libraries & public service counters
  • Recreational & public facilities
  • Shared public washrooms & common areas

For municipal, public-sector, and community-serving buildings.

Our Approach

Public buildings carry a different level of daily traffic.

A facility like a public service counter or a building with steady visitor and staff traffic (for example, a busier public office) often needs more frequent attention than a lower-traffic administrative space.

We factor in public access hours, visitor volume, and accountability requirements when building a public facility cleaning program.

Scheduling & Service Frequency

Frequency is based on how your facility is actually used.

Because public facilities are often used continuously throughout the day by both staff and the public, frequency is generally set higher for high-contact areas, with other areas cleaned on a set rotation.

Frequency Examples
Daily Service Common for facilities with continuous public access.
Multiple Daily Touchpoint Checks High-contact areas such as entrance doors and public washrooms may be checked more than once per day.
Weekly Rotations Lower-contact areas, such as some interior glass, cleaned on a rotating basis.
Custom Public-Hours Scheduling Service timed around public operating hours.
After-Hours Service Available where daytime cleaning would disrupt public access.
Documented Service Records Available where facilities require records for internal reporting.

These are examples only. Your actual cleaning frequency is confirmed during a site assessment, not assumed in advance.
